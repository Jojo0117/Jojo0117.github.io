A Communication-Efficient Protocol for Federated Learning in Energy Storage Systems
Accepted and to be published in Canadian Conference on Artificial Intelligence (CANAI) 2023
I analyze the data from 5 battery-backed energy storage systems. By visulizing the max and min value of the 5 clients in 50 communication rounds within 2.5 and 97.5 percentiles, the commonly-used single precision format can be reduced to 1.5 bytes representation, and thus decrease the transmission delay. The proposed communication protocal has wide applications on battery-backed energy storage systems, being validated through experiments on many datasets including the MNIST dataset. (Code) (Poster)
